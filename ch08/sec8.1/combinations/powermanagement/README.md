
## Exercise: From Energy Use, Optimisation, and Cost to Power Management

*This is a student exercise. No guided walkthrough is provided.*

```
Energy Use + Optimisation + Cost  ->  Power Management
```

### Your Task

Without being guided, derive why power management must exist as a
*designed discipline* rather than an afterthought.

Start here:
- Energy is not free. It is a cost--financial, thermal, and environmental.
- Computation consumes energy. More computation, more energy.
- Optimisation seeks to minimise cost while maintaining acceptable output.

Ask yourself:
1. What happens to a device with unlimited energy? Does power management matter?
2. At what point does energy become a constraint that shapes design decisions?
3. How does energy use interact with performance? Are they always in tension?
4. What mechanisms exist at the hardware level, OS level, and application level
   to manage power consumption?
5. What is the relationship between power management and latency?
   (Hint: think about what a CPU does when it is idle.)

Build a simple model. Simulate a device with a fixed energy budget running
tasks of varying intensity. Find the policy that maximises work done
within the budget.

Then name what you built.
